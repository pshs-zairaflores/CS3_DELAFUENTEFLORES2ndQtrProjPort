Ruth Esther D. Dela Fuente

Back when I was younger, I’ve always had these hyperfixations on superhero shows where characters had these super cool powers. It started with X-Men back in Grade 2, then Ben 10 in Grade 3, and then Avatar: The Last Airbender in Grades 4 and 5. My cousin first showed me this show during the pandemic on their Netflix, because all I could ever do was drown myself in my phone. Unfortunately, this hyperfixation of mine made me drown in my phone even more! I consumed all sorts of fanfiction, fanart, and Tumblr posts— which all thankfully brought back my spark in drawing.

It’s no joke when I say this show sort of saved me, and I’m glad I get to contribute to a project about the absolute masterpiece Avatar: The Last Airbender is.

Zaira Isabelle E. Flores

Ever since ABS-CBN got cancelled around 2020, I had to find another TV program to stay tuned to. I was then introduced to Netflix, a streaming site for movies and TV shows. And I finally found a masterpiece to watch, Avatar: The Last Airbender. The show entertained me throughout the whole lockdown. I watched the show for weeks until I finished all 3 seasons. Before that, I wasn't very interested in cartoons that have superheroes or superpowers in it as I thought they were too boyish. However, watching ATLA and the vast representation of the strong women characters changed my view.

ATLA became my top 1 favorite animated TV show during the pandemic, and it still is. Now, here I am making a website about it with my partner, which is super cool!
